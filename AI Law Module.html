Module description

This module explores the transformative impact of AI technologies on law firms' operations, ethics, and client services. Students will critically engage with current tools, future developments, and the socio-legal implications of automation in legal practice.

Module aims - intentions of the module

This module aims to:

  • Evaluate the potential and limitations of AI in legal practice
  • Identify key ethical and regulatory concerns around AI use
  • Analyse case studies of AI deployment in law firms
  • Propose informed strategies for responsible AI adoption

Intended Learning Outcomes (ILOs)

ILO: Module-specific skills

  • 1. Understand core applications of AI in legal contexts
  • 2. Critically assess challenges and benefits of AI implementation in law firms

ILO: Discipline-specific skills

  • 3. Apply legal reasoning to technological innovation
  • 4. Interpret relevant AI regulations and professional guidelines

ILO: Personal and key skills

  • 5. Communicate complex interdisciplinary ideas clearly
  • 6. Work collaboratively on practical problem-solving projects

Syllabus plan

  • Introduction to AI and Machine Learning
  • LegalTech ecosystems and startups
  • AI in contract review and discovery
  • Predictive analytics in litigation
  • Bias, ethics and fairness in AI
  • Data protection and compliance frameworks
  • Global regulation of AI in law
  • Real-world firm case studies and future trends
